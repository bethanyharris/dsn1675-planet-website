Discovery of Jupiter's Moons

The only spacecraft to actually orbit Jupiter was NASA’s Galileo mission, which went into orbit in 1995. NASA scientists were not satisfied with a few orbits of Jupiter. They wanted to see a bit more of the Jovian system, so Galileo was sent to observe a few moons. Galileo is credited as being the first spacecraft to observe a comet hitting a planet(Jupiter), first to flyby an asteroid, first to discover an asteroid with a moon, and it was the first to measure the crushing atmospheric pressure of Jupiter with a descent probe.

At the time that I’m writing this, Jupiter has a total of 63 named satellites.
